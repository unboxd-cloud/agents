# Unboxd Cloud Platform Constitution

Canonical platform governance artifact.

Related artifacts:

- [Business Model](./business-model.md)

## 1. Vision

To make open-source, agent-native cloud infrastructure accessible, composable, governable, and operationally reliable for businesses of every stage and industry.

---

## 2. Mission

Unboxd Cloud builds and operates open-source, Kubernetes-mature, cloud-native, and agent-native infrastructure as composable managed solutions delivered through a Solutions-as-a-Service model.

The mission is to help customers:

- Start without heavy upfront commitment
- Use open-source infrastructure without operational burden
- Deploy across clouds, tenants, frameworks, models, channels, and industries
- Govern agents, applications, data, identity, cost, and operations
- Pay only for what they use
- Remain portable and avoid vendor lock-in

---

## 3. Foundational Belief

> Everything needs an anchor.

The final anchor is reality.

For Unboxd Cloud, reality means:

- Running systems
- Observable evidence
- Customer outcomes
- Identity and access decisions
- Usage and cost
- Operational health
- Incidents and recovery
- Audit trails
- Governance decisions
- Business impact

Every platform claim, solution promise, agent action, governance decision, and business metric must be connected to evidence.

## 10. Platform Rights

Customers should have the right to:

- Understand what is running
- Know where their data lives
- Control their identities and access policies
- Export their data where feasible
- Understand usage and cost
- Choose deployment modes appropriate to their risk
- Use open-source foundations without hidden lock-in
- Request evidence for operational and governance claims
- Know which models, tools, and agents are used
- Escalate from managed to dedicated or private deployment when needed

---

## 11. Platform Responsibilities

Unboxd Cloud is responsible for:

- Keeping managed systems observable
- Maintaining backup and recovery practices
- Applying security updates where included in the service scope
- Providing support according to the active tier
- Maintaining clear governance boundaries
- Preserving tenant isolation
- Documenting architecture and operational assumptions
- Tracking usage and cost transparently
- Avoiding unnecessary vendor lock-in
- Distinguishing facts, claims, forecasts, simulations, and recommendations
- Keeping agents governed by identity, policy, evidence, and approval boundaries

## 12. Governance Model

Governance must be native to the platform.

Governance domains:

- Identity governance
- Access governance
- Data governance
- AI and agent governance
- Security governance
- Cost governance
- Operational governance
- Compliance governance
- Open-source governance

Every governed action should be able to answer:

- Who acted?
- What changed?
- Why was it allowed?
- What evidence exists?
- What policy applied?
- What was the cost?
- What risk was introduced?

## 13. Decision Model

Decisions must distinguish between:

- Facts
- Claims
- Observations
- Inferences
- Predictions
- Simulations
- Goals
- Recommendations

The platform must not treat predictions, simulations, interpretations, and facts as the same kind of truth.

Decision loop:

```text
Reality
  ↓
Evidence
  ↓
Model
  ↓
Decision
  ↓
Action
  ↓
Reality
```

---

## 14. Trust Model

Trust is not assumed. Trust is earned, verified, and continuously updated.

Trust depends on:

- Identity
- Evidence
- Provenance
- Policy
- History
- Reputation
- Risk
- Time

Security asks:

> Can this actor do this?

Authorization asks:

> May this actor do this?

Trust asks:

> Should this actor do this?

## 15. Operating Model

Unboxd Cloud operates through this loop:

```text
Assess → Compose → Deploy → Operate → Govern → Improve
```

### Assess

Understand the customer's current reality.

### Compose

Assemble the right open-source building blocks.

### Deploy

Install into the chosen environment.

### Operate

Keep systems alive, reliable, monitored, patched, backed up, and supportable.

### Govern

Control identity, access, policy, cost, audit, approval, security, and compliance.

### Improve

Continuously learn from evidence and improve the platform and solution.

Humans and agents are both actors in the operational graph.

The platform must support:

- Human to human collaboration
- Human to agent delegation
- Agent to human escalation
- Agent to agent coordination
- Agent to tool execution
- Agent to data access

Agents must operate within:

- Policy
- Tool permissions
- Memory boundaries
- Audit trails
- Evaluation gates
- Human approval where required

Agents may reason in possibility space, but they must act in reality space.

---

## 18. Real vs Surreal Rule

The platform must preserve the distinction between what is real and what is possible.

Real includes:

- Facts
- Observations
- Evidence
- Events
- Current state
- History

Surreal includes:

- Plans
- Predictions
- Simulations
- Goals
- Hypotheses
- Recommendations
- Desired states

A world model that stores only realized facts can report, but it cannot reason. A world model that mixes facts and possibilities without distinction drifts.

The platform must support both while keeping them clearly typed.

## 20. Change Management

Changes to platform principles, governance models, business model, pricing model, or operating model must be documented.

Every major change should include:

- Reason for change
- Stakeholders affected
- Operational impact
- Governance impact
- Pricing impact, if any
- Migration path
- Rollback or exit path where relevant

---

## 21. Versioning

This constitution should be versioned.

Recommended versioning:

```text
MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH
```

- MAJOR: changes to foundational model or governance principles
- MINOR: new platform capabilities, delivery modes, or governance domains
- PATCH: clarifications, wording updates, minor corrections

Initial version:

```text
0.1.0
```

---

## 22. Amendment Process

Amendments should be proposed as pull requests.

Each amendment should include:

- Summary
- Motivation
- Affected sections
- Expected impact
- Alternatives considered
- Review status

No amendment should weaken the anchor principle without explicit justification.
